## Conventions

- A **module** in Ade is a Cargo workspace crate (smallest independently-buildable unit). One exception: `ade_network` is split by *submodule color* — its BLUE submodules and its RED submodules are documented as two separate entries below, because `.idd-config.json` `core_paths` resolves BLUE at the submodule path level rather than crate-wide. The `ade_core::consensus` submodule sits *inside* the BLUE `ade_core` crate and is covered by that entry. The `ade_ledger::block_validity` / `ade_ledger::consensus_view` / `ade_ledger::tx_validity` / `ade_ledger::mempool::admit` / `ade_ledger::cert_classify` modules sit inside the BLUE `ade_ledger` crate and are covered by that entry; the RED `ade_ledger::consensus_input_extract` and the GREEN `ade_ledger::mempool::policy` sit inside the BLUE `ade_ledger` crate but carry a different color by their own module doc-comment and the cluster TCB Color Maps — they are surfaced as sub-classification notes inside the `ade_ledger` entry.

### `ade_codec`

> **Status change in PHASE4-B3.** `ade_codec` gained two new closed-grammar Conway decoders under `conway/`: `conway::cert` (`decode_conway_certs` — the Conway-complete certificate decoder, a closed grammar over CDDL tags `0..18` with `CodecError::UnknownCertTag` for tags ≥19, tags 5/6 decoding to the `ConwayCert::RemovedInConway` marker, and **no catch-all accept arm**) and `conway::withdrawals` (`decode_withdrawals` → `BTreeMap<RewardAccount, Coin>` plus `withdrawals_sum` → exact `i128`, rejecting a repeated map key with `CodecError::DuplicateMapKey` — never last-wins). Two new `CodecError` variants — `UnknownCertTag { tag, offset }` and `DuplicateMapKey { offset }` — were added; no new public `struct`/`enum` types.
>
> **Hardening in PHASE4-B3F.** `conway::cert::decode_conway_certs` now **rejects trailing bytes after the cert array** (`CodecError::TrailingBytes`, parity with `decode_withdrawals`) and **bounds its preallocation by the remaining input** rather than trusting the declared array count — so a huge declared count over a short buffer cannot over-allocate. Two regression tests cover both (`trailing_bytes_after_cert_array_rejected`, `huge_array_count_rejects_without_overallocating`).

### Naming convention

All crates are prefixed `ade_`. TCB color is not encoded in the crate name. The authoritative classifier is `.idd-config.json` `core_paths` plus the cluster doc TCB Color Maps for sub-crate scopes; CI scripts hard-code their BLUE list. The seven full-BLUE-scoped scripts (`ci_check_module_headers.sh`, `ci_check_no_semantic_cfg.sh`, `ci_check_no_signing_in_blue.sh`, `ci_check_hash_uses_wire_bytes.sh`, `ci_check_ingress_chokepoints.sh`, `ci_check_dependency_boundary.sh`, `ci_check_no_async_in_blue.sh`) use the full BLUE set: the 6 BLUE crates plus the 9 BLUE `ade_network` submodule paths. The B3 `ci_check_deposit_param_authority.sh` scopes the 6 BLUE crates (`BLUE_CRATES=(ade_codec ade_types ade_crypto ade_core ade_ledger ade_plutus)`). Four narrow scripts target `crates/ade_core/src/consensus/`. **No script narrowly scopes `crates/ade_ledger/src/tx_validity/`, `crates/ade_ledger/src/mempool/`, or `crates/ade_types/src/conway/`** — the B2/B3 closed-enum / closed-grammar properties on those paths rely on the full-BLUE scans plus named tests.
