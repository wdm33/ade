## Conventions

- **Source of rules.** The canonical, append-only source is the invariant
  registry at `.idd-config.json` `invariant_registry`
  (`docs/ade-invariant-registry.toml`). The registry was renamed from
  `constitution_registry.toml` in the post-Phase-4-N-D IDD-canonicalization
  delta (`ci_check_constitution_coverage.sh` was repointed in `39865f6`).
  Every `[[rules]]` entry whose `status` is not explicitly retired appears
  below. None are deprecated at HEAD.
- **Rule IDs** are stable across regenerations and immutable per
  registry doctrine. They are reproduced exactly as the registry holds them.
- **Families.** The registry groups rules under five family-letter
  prefixes:

  | Prefix | Family |
  |---|---|
  | `T-` | True invariants (project constitution §2) |
  | `DC-` | Derived Cardano compatibility invariants (§3) |
  | `CN-` | Classification-table invariants (`classification_table.md`) |
  | `RO-` | Release obligations (§4a) |
  | `OP-` | Operational invariants (§4b) |

  Within each family, level-3 sub-headings hold one rule each. The
  sub-section ID *is* the rule ID. Within a family, ordering follows
  the registry file order.
- **Four-cell contract.** Every rule carries the canonical four-row table:
  Source, Requirement, Code, Tests, CI. Where the registry has populated
  `code_locus`, `tests`, `ci_script`, those fields drive the row. A row
  marked `_(no enforcement found — gap)_` corresponds to a registry
  entry whose `status` is `declared` (no enforcement promised) or to a
  partial entry that has not yet pointed at a code/test/CI artifact.
- **Phase 4 N-A CI closure (PHASE4-N-A close).** Cluster N-A (Ouroboros mini-protocols, 11 protocols across N2N+N2C) strengthened four pre-existing registry rules with new tests against real cardano-node 11.0.1 captures and synthetic agency traces: `T-ENC-03` (+6 `*_real_capture_corpus::real_capture_round_trips_byte_identical` tests), `CN-WIRE-07` (+9 tests — 6 real-capture round-trip + 3 structural shape checks), `DC-PROTO-02` (+7 tests across chain-sync, block-fetch, keep-alive, peer-sharing), and `DC-PROTO-05` (+5 tests across N2N + N2C handshake). One new CI script — `ci/ci_check_ce_n_a_5_proof.sh` — exercises all 5 conditions of the CE-N-A-5 proof obligation per `docs/active/PHASE4-N-A_invariants.md` §6 (handshake negotiation deterministic, version gating across 11 protocols, real-capture round-trip byte-identicality, agency-trace transcript, malformed-frame canonical errors) and emits `docs/active/CE-N-A-5_evidence.toml` in the §7 #6 evidence schema. Two new registry IDs (`DC-CORE-01`, `DC-PROTO-06`) were added by the cluster — see their entries under §DC. Registry rules all remain `status = "declared"` — strengthenings populate the `tests` array and the `strengthened_in = ["PHASE4-N-A"]` cross-ref; the `ci_script` field on these four rules is still empty in the registry pending a follow-up commit, but `ci_check_ce_n_a_5_proof.sh` is linked from each rule's CI row below as the de-facto cluster gate.
- **Phase 4 N-D CI closure (`78da6c9`).** As of commit `78da6c9`
  (`chore(ci): close Phase 4 N-D CI gap — 3 new scripts, 9 rules enforced`),
  three new CI scripts were added — `ci/ci_check_chaindb_contract.sh`,
  `ci/ci_check_recovery_contract.sh`, `ci/ci_check_chaindb_crash_safety.sh`
  — wiring nine recovery/storage rules from "Code/Tests filled, no CI"
  partials into fully-enforced status. The flipped IDs are `T-REC-01`,
  `T-REC-02`, `DC-STORE-01`, `DC-STORE-02`, `DC-STORE-03`, `DC-STORE-05`,
  `CN-STORE-03`, `CN-STORE-04`, `CN-STORE-05`. `DC-STORE-04` remains
  `declared` by design — see its row and §B for the Tier 5 divergence
  note.
- **CI script BLUE scope (closed at `5b70bee`).** All seven BLUE-scoped
  CI scripts (`ci_check_module_headers.sh`, `ci_check_no_semantic_cfg.sh`,
  `ci_check_no_signing_in_blue.sh`, `ci_check_hash_uses_wire_bytes.sh`,
  `ci_check_ingress_chokepoints.sh`, `ci_check_dependency_boundary.sh`,
  `ci_check_forbidden_patterns.sh`) hard-code the full six-crate BLUE
  list `{ade_codec, ade_types, ade_crypto, ade_core, ade_ledger,
  ade_plutus}` declared by `.idd-config.json`. No `_(scope gap)_`

### B. `DC-STORE-04` Tier 5 divergence (documented, not a gap)

`DC-STORE-04` literally describes cardano-node's three-DB architecture
(ImmutableDB + VolatileDB + LedgerDB). Ade has deliberately diverged
per CE-79 Tier 5 (intentional-divergence on non-hash-critical
operator-facing surfaces, per `MEMORY.md::feedback_diverge_on_internal_surfaces.md`):
a single redb-backed store with logical separation via key prefixes
(`slots_by_hash`, `hashes_by_slot`, `snapshots_by_slot`) under
`crates/ade_runtime/src/chaindb/persistent.rs`.

The **semantic guarantees** that DC-STORE-04 was designed to deliver —
append-only finalized data and atomic snapshots — survive in Ade and
are now mechanically enforced under `DC-STORE-02` and `DC-STORE-03`
respectively, both flipped `declared → enforced` in `78da6c9`. The rule
remains `status = "declared"` in the registry rather than `enforced`
because Ade does not implement the literal three-DB topology DC-STORE-04
names; per IDD discipline, rules are append-only and may only be
strengthened, not weakened or repointed. A future `DC-STORE-04A`
strengthening (or a deprecation event paired with a major version bump)
is the legal way to reclassify.

This is recorded in the registry as a comment block above the
`DC-STORE-04` entry (commit `78da6c9`), in the `DC-STORE-04` row above,
and here in §B. **It does not read as an enforcement gap.** It is a
documented Tier 5 divergence with the semantic guarantee preserved
elsewhere and mechanically enforced.
